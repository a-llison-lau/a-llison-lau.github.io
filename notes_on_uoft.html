Notes on U of T



U of T is killing me. I always think that the school is too big that it fails to give anyone any sense of community. People are cold like the weather. It makes me feel helpless seeing how much the people I meet at this school tolerate incuriosity and intellectual laziness. Professors don’t look like they enjoyed teaching, no one pointing out errors in lab manuals written twenty years ago, no one cared to change it after pointing it out. Just as my thermal physics TA said, practical physics does not teach anything about practical physics, but rather practical life skills such as handling a lot of stress and terrible logistics. No wonder physics at u of t (aside from students lacking in math ability) never achieved anything great since a few decades ago. And how funny my friend’s friend got a research position at a liberal arts college yet I didn’t get any research at a research university.

I question the value of paying so much for such quality of education. And I question the meaning of university education. Today many people do not have a clear sense of their goals and purposes. At U of T exactly they are trained in hyper-specialised study that tell them how to do things but don’t ask them to think about why they should think of them, how to do them better, and don’t give them the chance to reflect on why they are here after all. Then after graduating they enter the world we all live in, which is a busy world. The tasks of setting up a career and a family, everything that distract them from the great questions of purpose and meaning. Systems pushing people to the verge of insanity, risking academic offence, dropping courses, taking easy ones to avoid a detrimental GPA. People’s souls being empty, and they haven’t even been given a moral vocabulary to help think these things through, hoping by piling success upon success they can fill the void from within. And they are the people from this value system who are running our world. This void in the people manifests itself in a kind of lostness, and I sometimes see it in myself and my friends.

When I graduate from this school, I would have learned nothing about my soul and its yearnings. Worse still, I would have lost my creativity and (hopefully not) myself to this game.

But perhaps something I have learned from this school is what it has not taught me, and perhaps the most significant thing I got from this school is knowing and becoming friends with 龔 who constantly wake me up from this chronic toxicity.

Oh, perhaps Stefano as well. The two people who actually know what they’re doing.
