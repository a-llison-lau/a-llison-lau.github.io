Inactive Projects

Here are some things I have been thinking to do. Yes I always have a lot of ideas but never have had time to do them, since there are simply too many! It is already very difficult to manage all the school work, not to mention these projects.

After watching David Attenborough's "Prehistoric Planet" and "Prehistoric Planet 2", I developed a deep appreciation for the field of paleontology. I am particularly drawn to the pterosaurs and ammonites, since I simply have an interest in flying things of all sorts, and computer graphics and aerodynamics are just two areas of study that fascinate me, which made this extra interesting to investigate upon. Pterosaurs are one of the most bizarre looking creatures every made on earth. I find it most interesting that pterosaurs have such a disproportionally long neck. So I'd like to investigate several key areas: wing shape and structure, flight adaptations, wing loading and flight performance, and aerodynamics.

I plan to ...

  • Develop a physics-based computational model that simulates the flight dynamics of pterosaurs, incorporating realistic wing morphologies based on fossil records and comparative anatomy.
  • Investigate the effects of various wing shapes, such as elongated versus broad wings, on the aerodynamic performance of pterosaurs, including lift generation, drag reduction, and maneuverability.
  • Analyze the relationship between wing loading, aspect ratio, and flight efficiency in different pterosaur species, considering their varying sizes and ecological niches.
  • Explore the role of wing flexibility and control surfaces in the maneuverability and stability of pterosaurs during flight, examining the interaction between wing kinematics and aerodynamic forces.
  • Visualize and analyze the simulated flight data using computer graphics techniques.

However, the difficult in this project is that I don't know anything about pterosaur fossils or any aerodynamics concepts. Aside from that, I don't know any professor or classmate who is familiar with this area. Thus to do this project, I need learn all these first, which will take a significant amount of time. It would take the time equivalent to an undergraduate research project. But I am still considering what undergraduate research project I should do.

As poor university students, we always have to walk around downtown, visiting Farm Boy, Loblaws, Metro, T&T, Longos, No Frills, Walmart, or whatever grocery stores nearby to find the cheapest deals. I want to write this website that compares food prices across grocery stores in downtown Toronto to help my friends, myself and my mom.

This website's main feature should be for the users to search their food item, say, eggs. The user could also select filters such as distance to the grocery store, opening hours, etc. The website then returns the the food items and the corresponding grocery store (and its location), sorted from cheapest to most expensive. This website should also have the feature of showing a map of grocery stores in downtown Toronto, where users can filter which brand they are looking for specifically.

However, this project would also take up a lot of time, because I am new to website development. First, I would have to figure out to collect then store the data from the official websites of these companies (which are updated at live time), then do some programming. This sounds a lot like my CSC207 course, except that I also need to deal with the data and the user interface. This would likely take up a term.
