Notes on U of T

Studying at U of T is suffocating. I constantly find myself questioning the school's immense size, which ultimately fails to foster a sense of community among its students. The people here often come across as distant and cold, much like the weather. It leaves me feeling helpless as I witness how many individuals at this institution tolerate a lack of curiosity and intellectual laziness. Professors don’t look like they enjoyed teaching, no one pointing out errors in lab manuals written twenty years ago, no one cared to change it after pointing it out. Just as my thermal physics TA said, practical physics does not teach anything about practical physics, but rather practical life skills such as handling a lot of stress and terrible logistics. No wonder physics at u of t (aside from students lacking in math ability) never achieved anything great since a few decades ago.

I find it crazy to invest a substantial amount of money in an education that may not deliver the expected quality. I contemplate the true essence of a university education. In today's world, many individuals lack a clear understanding of their goals and purposes. At U of T, students are immersed in highly specialized fields of study that focus primarily on teaching them how to execute tasks without fostering critical thinking about the underlying reasons or opportunities for improvement. They are deprived of the chance to reflect upon the purpose of their academic journey. Upon graduating, they enter a bustling world teeming with the demands of establishing a career and building a family, diverting their attention away from profound questions of purpose and meaning. The existing systems exert immense pressure on individuals, pushing them to the brink of mental strain and risking academic misconduct. Consequently, they may drop courses or opt for easier ones to safeguard their GPA. People's inner selves feel a sense of emptiness, as they lack the moral framework to navigate these existential dilemmas. Their hope lies in accumulating success after success, believing it will fill the void within them. This void experienced by people manifests itself as a pervasive feeling of being lost, which I occasionally observe in both myself and my friends.

When I graduate from this school, I would have learned nothing about my soul and its yearnings. Worse still, I would have lost my creativity and (hopefully not) myself to this game.

But maybe something I have learned from this school is what it has not taught me, and perhaps the most significant thing I got from this school is knowing and becoming friends with 龔 who constantly wake me up from this chronic toxicity.
