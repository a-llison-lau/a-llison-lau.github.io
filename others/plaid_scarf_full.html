Plaid Scarf

“Life can only be understood backwards; but it must be lived forwards.” – Soren Kierkegaard

A return to the love between two men as described in Plato’s Symposium, the story explores the ambiguity between friendship and relationship – who can love and what is love. This is a story of love, loss, redemption and sexual identity. Plaid scarf touches the readers’ hearts dearly by bringing the readers on a journey where two souls find where they should belong – the spark when the wall between them collapses, the fusion, and a tragic tear-apart. Readers will be surprised by the sensitivity, warmth and pureness of the friendship between two innocent boys.

Plaid Scarf begins with Ocean’s carol service rehearsal, which leads him back to emotional memories of Elliot. He envisions Elliot by his side at the organ and his delicate interpretation of the same piece.

Years ago, Ocean and Elliot were brought together by music. One day, Elliot disclosed his biggest weakness that Ocean never realised. They became each other’s confidant and shared lots of details of their lives with each other and their inner fear. Instead of hanging out with more friends, Elliot preferred to spend time alone with Ocean. [Add a short cycling story] Ocean started to ponder if Elliot’s feelings towards him was romantic.

As high school approaches to an end, Elliot planned to study overseas. Ocean could not bear being separated from Elliot, and deliberately found something to argue with him. Eventually, the two grew apart.

The broken friendship became a source of pain and self-evaluation for Ocean, who laments that his presence has no significance other than to ruin others and that he shouldn’t love anyone other than himself, whereas Elliot moved on to find new love. Ocean realized that their encounter has already got its full meaning.
